Finite Impulse Response Filter
Using of Fourier Transform in FIR
-
Suppose signal x(n) is the sum of two signals \(x_1\)(n) and \(x_2\)(n). In which \(x_1\)(n) is a cosine signal with an angular frequency of \(ω_1\) rad/s, \(x_2\)(n) is also a cosine signal with an angular frequency of \(ω_2\) rad/s. People use an FIR high/low pass filter with a pulse response length of 3 with the assumption h(0) = h(2) = α and h(1) = β to suppress the \(x_i\)(n) signal and give completely passes through signal \(x_j\)(n).
